Can Google Play Points be shared with family?

Updated July 24, 2026 · US guide

No. Play Points cannot be transferred, gifted, or combined between Google Accounts, even inside a Google family group. Family Library can share certain purchased content, but it does not turn individual point balances into a family balance.

Who earns points with a family payment method?

The Google Account that makes the eligible purchase earns the points. Google's troubleshooting guidance says you do not earn points from purchases made by someone else in the family group, even when they use a family payment method.

Before checkout, confirm the profile icon and account email. Paying with the family card does not move the points to the family manager.

Family Library is different

Family Library can share eligible purchased apps, games, movies, TV shows, ebooks, and audiobooks with up to five family members. Eligibility varies by content and country. In-app purchases and subscriptions generally are not the same as a shareable app purchase.

A shared title remains content access; it does not share the buyer's Play Points, redeemed Play credit, coupons, or level.

A safer family setup

  1. Decide which account should own the purchase before paying.
  2. Check whether the item shows Family Library eligibility.
  3. Confirm the active Google Account at checkout.
  4. After purchase, verify points in that account's Points history.

Avoid passing passwords between family members. Use Google's family features and purchase approvals instead.

FAQ

Can I send my points to a family member?

No. Google does not provide point transfers or account combining, including within a family group.

Who earns points with a family payment method?

The account making the eligible purchase earns the points, not automatically the family manager.

Does Family Library share Play credit?

No. Family Library shares eligible content, not Play Points balances or redeemed Play credit.
